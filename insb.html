Project report: Solid State Synthesis of Indium Antimonide

Aim

Synthesize InSb semiconductor via solid-state synthesis, using solid Indium (In) and Antimony (Sb) pieces as precursors, and to deposit a thin film using the TVD technique.

Synthesis process

The precursors are ground in an agate mortar and pestle for 1h to get a homogeneous mixture then form a pallet. Place the pallet inside box furnace. Heat the furnace so that both atoms can diffuse and form the solid solution. Do the annealing and we get pallet of InSb. Place the pallet inside the TVD chamber then deposit film using TVD

Characterization

Bulk sample was characterized using X-ray diffraction and four probe method is used to find the band gap of the Indium antimonide sample. Thin film was used to calculate the hall voltage using the hall effect and to find the grain size and thickness of the film, SEM was performed. To find the composition EDAX was performed. XRD was also performed on the as-deposited thin film of the InSb sample. The plot shown in figure 5 shows some sharp peaks but also one broad distribu- tion which signifies that the film has some amorphous nature to overcome that, annealing should be performed, which makes the molecule mobile and allow it to take the specific place and form a lattice which makes the film crystalline and then sharp peaks will be obtained since lattice is formed.

Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) was performed on a thin film to determine the grain size of InSb and thickness of the film. The resolution was 500 nm and the magnification was 100000X at the time of measuring grain sizes, the sizes ranged from 70 nm to 115 nm. The thickness was measured using cross sample holder so that the electron beam hit the film side way under the resolution of 5 μm and the magnification was 20000x. The thickness measured was 2 μm to 2.25 μm.

EDAX was performed to get the elemental composition (by weight) of Indium and Antimony from the Indium antimonide. It was found to be 47.8 % of Indium and 52.2 % of Antimony in Indium antimonide (by weight) as shown in fig 8b. Mole percent is 49.2 % of In and 50.8 % of Sb.

Results and Discussion

Indium antimonide was successfully prepared using the solid-state synthesis. We found the band gap of the sample as 0.158 eV which is close to the actual band gap of 0.17 eV. Since the band gap of InSb is in the range of infrared light, it can be used in the photodetector of infrared light on in some thermal imaging devices.

XRD was performed on the sample which shows the peak and when peaks were matched with JCPDs data. It shows that most of the reactants were converted to product and crystalline InSb is formed with a very small amount of precursor left as shown in XRD here two small unmarked peaks corresponding to precursors.

EDAX confirms the presence of equimolar content of Indium and Antimony in the final compound and that confirms the stoichiometry of the sample as InSb.

Grain size of the deposited sample in the film is in the range from 70 nm to 115 nm.
